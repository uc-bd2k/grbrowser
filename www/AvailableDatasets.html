LINCS Pilot Phase Joint Project:

About this dataset

The dose-dependent sensitivities of 6 nonmalignant breast and breast cancer cell lines were assayed by the HMS LINCS Center by measuring cell counts 72 hours after treatment with each of 107 small molecule inhibitors across a 6-point dose range. For further information about the assay and to download this dataset (#20252) or the cell count datasets from which the metrics were calculated (#20245 to #20251), please visit the HMS LINCS Database.

LINCS Phase II MCF10A Common Project:

About this dataset

As a pilot study for the LINCS MCF 10A Common Project, the dose-dependent sensitivity of a common isolate of the nonmalignant breast cell line MCF 10A was assayed independently by 4 of the LINCS Data and Signature Generation Centers by measuring cell counts or by estimating proliferation using the CellTiter-Glo® assay 72 hours after treatment with each of 8 small molecule inhibitors across a 9-point dose range.

HMS LINCS Reanalysis of Heiser et al. (2012):

About this dataset

The dose-dependent sensitivities of 74 nonmalignant breast and breast cancer cell lines were assayed by estimating proliferation using the CellTiter-Glo® assay 72 hours after treatment with each of ~100 small molecule inhibitors across a 9-point dose range. These data were collected by the laboratory of Dr. Joe Gray (Oregon Health Sciences University), and analysis of the data using traditional growth inhibition metrics (GI50) was reported in Heiser et al. (2012). Here, the HMS LINCS Center reports the GR metrics for the data.

HMS LINCS Seeding Density Project:

About this dataset

The density- and context-dependent sensitivities of 6 breast cancer cell lines plated at 6 different cell densities were assayed by the HMS LINCS Center by measuring cell counts 72 hours after treatment with each of 12 small molecule inhibitors across a 9-point dose range. For further information about the assay and to download the GR metrics dataset (#20258) or the cell count datasets from which the metrics were calculated (#20256 and #20257), please visit the HMS LINCS Database.
